Who we are

Vandy Oil Co (VOC) is an oil and natural gas company with activities in exploration, production, refining and exporting oil as well as exploration and production of natural gas. Its headquarters is located in Nashville, TN.

Some Oil Industry Definitions

Reservoir: Porous and permeable rock unit with well-defined boundaries containing commercial amounts of hydrocarbons.
Well: A drilled cased borehole communicating the reservoir with surface installations. It’s a conduit. Serves the purpose of bringing oil or other hydrocarbons - such as natural gas - to the surface.
Rate: Change of a variable with respect to time. Some examples seen here are:
Oil rate: barrels of oil per day (BOPD)
Gas rate: thousands of standard cubic feet per day (MSCF/D)
Water rate: barrels of water per day (BWPD)
Water cut: The ratio or percentage of water in total liquids.
Gas-Oil ratio (GOR): The fraction of gas volume over oil.
Cumulative production: Defined as the gross amount of oil and gas production from an oil reservoir over a particular period of time of the life of a well or reservoir.

Our Story

The BCS oil field was discovered in November 1953 with the drilling of wells BCS0006 and BCS0007. At that time the VOC decided to test those wells for an extended period of time in order to capture the most of relevant reservoir data.
Commercial production began in 1971 when well BCS0018 was drilled. This proved the real potential of the reservoirs. A total of 44 producing wells were drilled in the field.
The water production in the Eastern part of the field began in September 1987, specifically in well BCS0031. Today’s water cut (the ratio of water in total fluids) is about 75%. In 2007 a water injection pilot project was conducted in a neighbor well to BCS0031, causing a production of injection water in BCS0031. This was the first time that VOC thought that BCS could be a naturally fractured field.

BCS Oil Field Description

The geological productive formation is a sequence of sandstone–shale rocks from the Eocene age. At BCS Oil Field, we have identified 6 different reservoirs separated by geological faults, impermeable rocks(seals) and fluid contacts. The main characteristics of this oil field are a)the presence of an active aquifer and b)some reservoirs are partially naturally fractured.
The main average properties of the oil fields are:
Depth: 9500 feet
API gravity: 21°
Porosity: 7%
Permeability: 4 md Driven mechanism: Hydraulic, Solution gas
Lifting methods: Gas-lift, sucker rod beam pumping, electro-submersible pumping, progressive cavity pumping
